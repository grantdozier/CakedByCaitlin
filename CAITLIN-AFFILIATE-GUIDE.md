# How to get paid for the products you already recommend

You already tell brides what to buy. Every day. For free.

An affiliate account means that when someone buys a product from a link on your site, the shop pays you a cut. The bride pays exactly the same price. Nothing is added on. The shop just shares its money with you instead of keeping all of it.

You don't have to sell anything. You don't have to hold stock. You just have to be linked properly.

There are four programs worth knowing about. **Do them in this order.** The order matters — the first one covers most of your list in a single application, and the second one has a deadline that starts ticking the day you sign up.

## 2. Amazon Associates — the one you asked for

You don't have this yet and you want it. Good. It's free, anyone can sign up, and **there is no follower minimum at all.**

### ⚠️ Read this part twice

**You must make 3 qualifying sales within 180 days, or Amazon closes your account.**

That is the trap. People sign up, get excited, forget about it, and six months later the account is gone. Three sales. Six months. That's the whole test.

It is not a hard test — three brides buying a $12 beauty sponge clears it. But it will not happen by itself. **Don't sign up for Amazon until you're ready to actually send people links.**

If it does get closed, it's not the end of the world — you can reapply. But it's a hassle, so let's just pass it the first time.

## 3. Levanta — a better-paying Amazon link, for some brands

Levanta is another way to link to Amazon. The difference: the **brand** pays you directly, and it usually pays a lot better than Amazon's own 3%.

### ⚠️ It is one or the other. Never both.

You may read older blog posts saying you can earn a Levanta commission *and* an Amazon commission on the same sale. **That is out of date, and following it now can get your Amazon account closed.**

Amazon changed the rules on **20 December 2024**: you may not earn from two programs on the same click, and you must **never put your Amazon tag on a Levanta link.**

So the real rule is simple, and it's per product:

- Product **is** on Levanta → use the **Levanta** link. (Better rate. This is the win.)
- Product is **not** on Levanta → use your normal **Amazon** link.

Never both on the same product. I handle this in the site's settings — you never have to think about which is which, and I will never put a link on your site that breaks this rule.

- Levanta is **free** for creators.
- It only works for brands that have signed up with Levanta.
- Rates come from the brand and are typically much higher than Amazon's 3%.

**What to do:** get Amazon set up first. Then sign up at levanta.io, search their brand list for the Amazon products you actually recommend, and **tell me which ones show up.** I'll switch those specific products over to Levanta links and take the Amazon tag off them.

## What you're legally required to say

Short version: **if you earn money when someone buys, you have to tell them.**

This isn't optional and it isn't a formality. The FTC (the US consumer watchdog) requires the disclosure to sit **right next to the links** — where someone reading normally cannot miss it. Tucked in tiny grey text at the bottom of the page is not good enough, legally.

Amazon goes further: they require this exact sentence, word for word:

> **As an Amazon Associate I earn from qualifying purchases.**

You can't reword it. You can't shorten it.

### The good news: your website already does this for you

Both lines are already on the site, automatically, above the first affiliate link on every page that has one. You do not have to think about it, remember it, or type it. It's handled.

### The part that IS on you: Instagram and TikTok

**When you post these links on social, you have to disclose there too.** The website can't do that for you — those posts belong to you, not the site.

It's easy. Any of these work:

- `#ad`
- `commissionable link`
- `affiliate link — I earn a small commission`
- "If you buy through my link I make a little commission, at no extra cost to you"

Put it **in the caption near the top**, not buried after fifteen hashtags. Say it out loud in the video too if you're talking about the product.

That's the whole rule. Say it plainly, say it early, and you're fine.
